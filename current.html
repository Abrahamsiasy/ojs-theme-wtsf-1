Mathematical Structures Underlying Tai-Chi, the Lo Shu Square, and Fibonacci Sequences

Bill C. Drougas · Theodora Tyrovola

We examine deep mathematical connections between Taijiquan form geometry, the classical Lo Shu magic square, and the Fibonacci sequence — proposing a unifying numerical framework linking ancient Chinese cosmological models with modern symmetry theory.

"Taiji Master": A Computational Framework for Movement Pattern Recognition

Shudong Li · Ming-Yu Chen · Wei Zhang

Multi-modal AI framework combining pose estimation, biomechanical modeling, and gesture classification to objectively evaluate Taijiquan form quality. Validated across 240 practitioners spanning beginner-to-master skill levels.

Clinical Trial

Tao Calligraphy Practice as Adjunctive Therapy for Major Depressive Disorder: A Randomized Controlled Trial

Harold J. Recinos · Solvason H. · Yunita W.

Eight-week mindful brush-stroke practice produced statistically significant reductions in HAM-D scores (p<0.01) compared to standard-care controls. Findings suggest meditative calligraphy may complement conventional treatment.

EEG / Neurophysiology

Electrophysiological Correlates of Post-Standing Meditation in Long-Term Taiji Practitioners

Weimo Zhu · Wenguo Ma · L. Tibebu

Quantitative EEG of 32 long-term practitioners reveals significant elevation of frontal alpha and theta power during post-standing zhan zhuang practice, suggesting altered states distinct from baseline rest or focused-attention meditation.
