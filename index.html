Mathematical Structures Underlying Tai-Chi, the Lo Shu Square, and Fibonacci Sequences

Bill C. Drougas · Theodora Tyrovola

We examine the deep mathematical connections between Taijiquan form geometry, the classical Lo Shu magic square, and the Fibonacci sequence — proposing a unifying numerical framework that links ancient Chinese cosmological models with modern symmetry theory and recursive structures.

Shudong Li · Ming-Yu Chen · Wei Zhang

A multi-modal AI framework combining pose estimation, biomechanical modeling, and gesture classification to objectively evaluate Taijiquan form quality. Validated across 240 practitioners spanning beginner-to-master skill levels.

Tao Calligraphy Practice as Adjunctive Therapy for Major Depressive Disorder: A Randomized Controlled Trial

Harold J. Recinos · Solvason H. · Yunita W.

Eight-week mindful brush-stroke practice produced statistically significant reductions in HAM-D scores (p<0.01) compared to standard-care controls. Findings suggest meditative calligraphy may complement conventional treatment.

EEG / Neurophysiology

Electrophysiological Correlates of Post-Standing Meditation in Long-Term Taiji Practitioners

Weimo Zhu · Wenguo Ma · L. Tibebu

Quantitative EEG of 32 long-term practitioners reveals significant elevation of frontal alpha and theta power during post-standing zhan zhuang practice, suggesting altered states distinct from baseline rest or focused-attention meditation.

Aims & Scope

Research areas we publish

Practice & Pedagogy

Empirical studies of Taijiquan and Qigong training methods, instructor effectiveness, and skill-acquisition models.

Clinical & Integrative

Randomized trials, meta-analyses, and case studies on Taiji as adjunct therapy for mental and physical health conditions.

Neuroscience & EEG

Brain imaging, electrophysiology, and cognitive studies on meditative movement, attention, and altered states.

Biomechanics & AI

Motion capture, pose estimation, computational modeling, and machine-learning approaches to movement analysis.
