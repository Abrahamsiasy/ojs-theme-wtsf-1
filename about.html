About the Journal

Advancing rigorous, cross-disciplinary research on Taiji, Qigong, and integrative mind-body science since 2022.

Our Mission

Bridging tradition and empirical science

The Journal of Taiji Science (JTS) is an international, bilingual, peer-reviewed open-access journal published annually by the World Taiji Science Federation (WTSF). Founded in 2022, JTS provides a rigorous platform for empirical and theoretical research at the intersection of traditional movement disciplines and contemporary scientific inquiry.

We welcome contributions from neuroscience, kinesiology, integrative medicine, biomechanics, computational modeling, sport pedagogy, and the humanities — anywhere that careful investigation can illuminate the practice, mechanisms, or outcomes of Taijiquan and related arts.

Aims & Scope

Research areas we publish

JTS publishes original research, systematic reviews, clinical trials, computational studies, theoretical essays, and historical scholarship across the following domains:

Practice & Pedagogy

Training methods, instructor effectiveness, skill acquisition, learning curves, and forms standardization.

Clinical Research

RCTs and observational studies on Taiji as adjunctive therapy for cardiovascular, neurological, and mood disorders.

Neuroscience

EEG, fMRI, biomarker, and cognitive studies of meditative movement and attentional states.

Biomechanics

Motion capture, force analysis, joint kinematics, postural sway, and balance research.

Computational Modeling

AI, machine learning, pose estimation, and computer-vision approaches to movement assessment.

History & Philosophy

Textual studies, cross-cultural transmission, lineage research, and conceptual analyses of classical sources.

Editorial Policy

Peer review & open access

Double-blind review

All submissions undergo rigorous double-blind peer review by at least two independent reviewers drawn from our international expert pool. Average decision time: 8–12 weeks.

Diamond open access

All articles published under CC BY 4.0. APC waivers available for authors from low- and middle-income countries. No subscription fees, no paywalls.
